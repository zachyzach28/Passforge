Understanding password security helps protect all your online accounts. Here are the essentials.

🔐

What Makes a Strong Password?

Length, variety, and randomness. A strong password is 16+ characters and mixes uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols — with no dictionary words or personal information.

📏

Why Length Matters Most

Every added character multiplies the possible combinations exponentially. A 20-character password is astronomically harder to crack than a 12-character one, even if less "complex."

♻️

Never Reuse Passwords

When one site is breached, attackers test those credentials on thousands of others (credential stuffing). One reused password can expose all your accounts at once.

🗄️

Use a Password Manager

You can't memorize 100 unique strong passwords. A manager like Bitwarden or 1Password generates, stores, and auto-fills them securely — you only need to remember one master password.

📱

Enable Two-Factor Auth (2FA)

2FA adds a second step after your password. Even if your password leaks, an attacker can't log in without your phone, hardware key, or biometric.

⚡

How Attackers Crack Passwords

Methods include brute force, dictionary attacks, and credential stuffing. Modern GPUs test billions of hashes per second — short or common passwords fall in seconds.
